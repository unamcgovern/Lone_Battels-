Lone Battles: A Data-Driven Exploration of Child Deprivation

This dashboard, designed with the intent of shedding light on the silent plight of children facing hardships, revolves around a central theme: The percentage of children with exactly one deprivation.

UNICEF defines child deprivations as conditions that deny children access to basic human rights and necessities, such as education, nutrition, water, sanitation, shelter, and health.

A child suffering from exactly one deprivation faces significant challenges that could affect their development, education, and future potential.

1. Map Visualisation

The map displays the percentage of children with exactly one deprivation across various countries, the population of each country is also included.

This visualisation offers a global overview, highlighting geographical patterns and pinpointing regions where this issue is most prevalent.

Users can instantly grasp which countries are facing the most significant challenges and observe variations across different parts of the world.

3. Life Expectancy (Bar Chart)

The dashboard then narrows its focus to the top 10 countries with the highest average percentage of children suffering from at least one deprivation, presenting a bar chart of their average life expectancy.

This visualisation starkly illustrates the long-term impacts of child deprivation on health and longevity, offering a sobering reminder of the stakes involved.

4. Population Increase (Line Chart)

Complementing the bar chart is a line chart showcasing the population increase in these top 10 countries.

This chart provides insights into how growing populations may exacerbate the challenges of addressing child deprivations, highlighting the urgent need for scalable solutions.

Iraq

Iraq has the highest percentage of children suffering from exactly one deprivation, several factors contirbute to this distinction including the eyars of conflict, political instability, economic challenges and displacement.

These conditions have severely impacted infrastructure, access to basic sercices, and the overall quality of life, palcing children at a higher risk of experiencing deprivation. The lack of stable education and helath facilities, couples with inadequate water and sanitation systems.

Conclusion

This dashboard not only serves as a tool for awareness but also as a call to action. By visualising the data on child deprivation, it underscores the importance of targeted interventions, international cooperation, and sustainable development efforts to address the root causes of these deprivations.

This story invites policymakers and the global community to reflect on the the well-being of children worldwide, urging a collective response to ensure every child has the opportunity to thrive.
